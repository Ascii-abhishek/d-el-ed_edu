Educational Evaluation, Action Research and Innovation — Introduction

This subject teaches you how to assess learning effectively, conduct small-scale classroom research, and bring innovation into your teaching practice.

Key Areas Covered

  • Types of evaluation: formative and summative
  • Assessment tools: observation, portfolios, tests, rubrics
  • Action research: meaning, steps, and classroom use
  • Identifying a classroom problem and designing a study
  • Innovation in teaching: what it means and examples
  • Report writing for action research

Why It Matters

Teachers who reflect on and study their own classrooms continuously improve. Action research makes you a thinking, evidence-based teacher.
