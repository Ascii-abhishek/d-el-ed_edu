Extraction of Metals: Easy Flow

Ore from mine
     ↓
Crushing and grinding (reduce to powder)
     ↓
Concentration (remove gangue by froth flotation / gravity / magnetic separation)
     ↓
Roasting / Calcination (heat ore in air to convert to oxide)
     ↓
Reduction (remove oxygen using coke/carbon → crude metal)
     ↓
Refining (electrolytic refining → pure metal)
     ↓
Useful pure metal

Steps Explained

  • Mining: Ore taken out from earth. Open-cast (surface) or underground methods.
  • Crushing and grinding: Large ore pieces broken into fine powder.
  • Concentration: Gangue (soil, sand, clay) removed. Methods: froth flotation (sulphide ores), gravity separation (dense metals), magnetic separation (magnetic ores like iron).
  • Roasting: Sulphide ore heated in excess air → converts to oxide. Removes sulphur as SO₂.
  • Calcination: Carbonate ore heated in absence of air → converts to oxide. Removes CO₂.
  • Reduction: Metal oxide + coke (carbon) → metal + CO₂. This removes oxygen and gives crude metal.
  • Refining: Crude metal purified. Most important method: electrolytic refining (crude metal as anode, pure metal as cathode, metal salt as electrolyte).

Important Exceptions — Must Remember

  • Mercury: Only metal that is liquid at room temperature.
  • Graphite: Non-metal (carbon) but conducts electricity — used in electrodes.
  • Iodine: Non-metal but lustrous (shiny) — like a metal in appearance.
  • Sodium and potassium: Metals but very soft — can be cut with a knife; stored in kerosene.
  • Gallium and caesium: Metals that melt slightly above room temperature.

Classroom Activity

  • Show: iron nail, copper wire, aluminium foil, pencil graphite, chalk, sulphur.
  • Ask students to test each for: shine, bending, circuit conduction (battery + bulb), sound when tapped.
  • Record in a table: Metal or Non-metal? This builds observational skills over rote memory.
  • Connect: why is copper used for wires (ductile + best conductor among common metals)?

One-minute Revision

  • Ore = useful mineral for profitable metal extraction.
  • Gangue = unwanted impurities in ore; removed during concentration.
  • Extraction: mining → crushing → concentration → reduction → refining.
  • Metals: shiny, malleable, ductile, sonorous, good conductors — exceptions: mercury (liquid), sodium (soft).
  • Non-metals: dull, brittle, poor conductors — exceptions: graphite (conducts), iodine (shiny).
  • All ores are minerals; NOT all minerals are ores.

Common Exam Lines

  • All ores are minerals, but all minerals are not ores.
  • The removal of gangue from ore is called concentration of ore.
  • Metals are used widely because they are strong, conductive, and can be shaped (malleable and ductile).
  • Steel is stronger than pure iron because carbon fills the gaps between iron atoms, preventing them from sliding.
  • Electrolytic refining gives the purest form of metal.
