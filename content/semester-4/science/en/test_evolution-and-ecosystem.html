Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write the meaning of ecosystem with one example.

An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living components (plants, animals, microbes) interact with non-living components (air, water, soil, sunlight, temperature). Example: a pond, a forest, or a school garden.

2. Give two differences between food chain and food web.
Food ChainFood Web
Single, straight path of energyMany interconnected food chains
Simple and easy to drawMore realistic and complex
3. What is adaptation? Give one example.

Adaptation is a feature or behaviour that helps an organism survive and reproduce better in its environment. Example: thick fur of animals in cold regions helps them retain body heat.

4. Name any four abiotic components of an ecosystem.

Sunlight, water, air (oxygen and CO₂), soil, and temperature are the main abiotic components.

5. What is the role of decomposers in an ecosystem?

Decomposers such as bacteria and fungi break down dead organic matter into simple substances and return nutrients to the soil. They maintain nutrient cycling and keep the ecosystem clean.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the components of an ecosystem using a school garden as an example.

Definition: An ecosystem has biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components that interact with each other.

In a school garden:

  • Producers: Grass, flowering plants, neem tree — make food by photosynthesis.
  • Primary consumers: Ants, butterflies, caterpillars — eat plants.
  • Secondary consumers: Birds, lizards — eat insects.
  • Decomposers: Bacteria and fungi in soil — break dead leaves and return nutrients.
  • Abiotic: Sunlight, air, water, soil temperature.

All these components depend on each other, forming a balanced system.

2. Draw and explain a food chain and a food web.

Food Chain: A straight path showing who eats whom.

Sun → Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake → Eagle

Energy passes from producer to consumers step by step. Some energy is lost as heat at each step.

Food Web: Several food chains connected together.

Grass → Grasshopper → Frog → Snake
  |           ↓              ↓
  ↓       Small bird →    Eagle
 Goat

In a food web, one organism may eat many things and be eaten by many. It is more realistic than a single food chain.

3. What is Darwin's theory of evolution? Explain natural selection.

Darwin's Theory of Evolution:

  • All life on Earth shares a common ancestor and has changed over time.
  • Organisms that are better suited to their environment survive and reproduce.
  • This process is called Natural Selection.

Natural Selection — key points:

  • In a population, individuals have slight variations.
  • The environment selects those with useful traits.
  • Useful traits are passed to offspring generation after generation.
  • Overtime, populations change — this is evolution.
  • Example: bacteria become resistant to antibiotics through natural selection.
