Mensuration

Mensuration is one of the most formula-heavy and exam-friendly chapters. In Semester 4, questions usually come from cuboid, cylinder, cone, and trapezium. The chapter becomes easy if you identify the figure correctly before applying the formula.

1. Core Formulas

ShapeFormulaWhere used
Cuboid volume\(V = l \times b \times h\)Box, room, brick
Cylinder volume\(V = \pi r^2 h\)Pipe, drum, glass
Cone curved surface area\(\text{CSA} = \pi r l\)Cap, conical tent, hat
Trapezium area\(A = \frac{1}{2}(a+b)h\)Field, land strip, diagram-based question

2. Important Idea: Rectangle Rotated Around a Side

If a rectangle of length 10 cm and breadth 7 cm is rotated around its length, it forms a cylinder.

  • Height of cylinder = 10 cm
  • Radius of cylinder = 7 cm

So the volume is:

\[ V = \pi r^2 h = \pi \times 7^2 \times 10 = 490\pi \text{ cm}^3 \]

If \(\pi = \frac{22}{7}\), then

\[ V = 490 \times \frac{22}{7} = 1540 \text{ cm}^3 \]

3. Worked Example: Cone Curved Surface Area

Question: In a cone, \(VO = 15\) cm and \(OB = 8\) cm. Find the curved surface area.

  • Here radius \(r = 8\) cm
  • Slant height \(l = 15\) cm

\[ \text{CSA} = \pi r l = \pi \times 8 \times 15 = 120\pi \]

If \(\pi = \frac{22}{7}\), then

\[ \text{CSA} = \frac{2640}{7} \text{ cm}^2 \]

4. Worked Example: Trapezium Area

Question: Parallel sides of a trapezium are 25 cm and 12 cm, and the distance between them is 8 cm. Find the area.

\[ A = \frac{1}{2}(a+b)h \]

\[ A = \frac{1}{2}(25+12)\times 8 \]

\[ A = \frac{1}{2}\times 37 \times 8 = 148 \text{ cm}^2 \]

5. Conical Cap / Hat Type Question

These questions usually give cloth area and circumference of the circular edge.

  • Use edge circumference to find radius: \(2\pi r\)
  • Use cloth area as curved surface area: \(\pi r l\)
  • Then find slant height \(l\)

6. Common Mistakes

  • Using diameter directly as radius.
  • Applying cylinder formula when the figure is a cone.
  • For trapezium, adding only one parallel side instead of both.
  • Confusing slant height \(l\) with vertical height \(h\).

7. Quick Figure Guide

Cuboid    -> box shape
Cylinder  -> round pipe / drum
Cone      -> ice-cream cone / cap
Trapezium -> one pair of parallel sides

8. Classroom Connection

  • Use chalk box for cuboid, bottle for cylinder, birthday cap for cone.
  • Ask students to identify shapes from classroom objects first, then attach formulas.
  • Connect land measurement and water storage examples to daily life.

One-Minute Revision

  • Cuboid volume \(= lbh\)
  • Cylinder volume \(= \pi r^2 h\)
  • Cone curved surface area \(= \pi r l\)
  • Trapezium area \(= \frac{1}{2}(a+b)h\)
  • Always identify radius, height, and slant height correctly.

Common Exam Lines

  • If a rectangle revolves around one of its sides, the solid formed is a cylinder.
  • The curved surface area of a cone depends on radius and slant height.
  • The area of a trapezium is half the product of height and sum of parallel sides.
