Statistics: Median and Mode

Scoring chapter for direct questions. In Semester 4, statistics usually appears in a very practical form: arrange the observations, then find the median or mode. If the steps are neat, this chapter is quick to finish in the exam.

1. Basic Ideas

  • Mode tells us which value appears most often.
  • Median tells us the middle position of data.
  • Median depends on order, mode depends on frequency.
  • Before finding median, always arrange data from smaller to bigger value.

2. Formula Corner

CaseFormulaMeaning
\(n\) oddMedian = \(\left(\frac{n+1}{2}\right)\)th termPick the single middle term
\(n\) evenMedian = average of \(\frac{n}{2}\)th and \(\frac{n}{2}+1\)th termsTake mean of the two middle terms

3. Worked Example: Median

Question: Find the median of 148, 151, 153, 145, 143, 152, 155, 163, 169.

Step 1: Arrange in ascending order

\[ 143, 145, 148, 151, 152, 153, 155, 163, 169 \]

Step 2: Count observations \(n=9\)

Step 3: Middle position

\[ \frac{n+1}{2} = \frac{9+1}{2} = 5 \]

Answer: Median = 5th term = 152

4. Worked Example: Mode

Question: Find the mode of 12, 13, 14, 14, 10, 12, 14, 18, 14, 20, 12, 13.

5. Even Number Example

Data: 5, 8, 4, 10, 12, 7

Ordered data: 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, 12

Here \(n=6\), so median is average of 3rd and 4th terms:

\[ \text{Median} = \frac{7+8}{2} = \frac{15}{2} = 7.5 \]

7. Common Mistakes

  • Finding median without arranging the data first.
  • Choosing the numerically largest value instead of the most repeated value for mode.
  • For even \(n\), forgetting to take the average of the two middle terms.
  • Making counting mistakes while locating the middle term.

8. Classroom Connection

  • Use real class data such as attendance, marks, or height of students.
  • Ask children to stand in height order to physically show the median child.
  • Use favorite fruit or color survey to show mode in a fun way.

One-Minute Revision

  • Median = middle value after arranging the data.
  • Mode = most frequent value.
  • Odd \(n\): one middle term.
  • Even \(n\): average of two middle terms.
  • Median needs order; mode needs frequency check.

Common Exam Lines

  • Median is a positional average.
  • Mode is the value with maximum frequency.
  • For ungrouped data, arrange the observations in ascending or descending order before finding the median.
