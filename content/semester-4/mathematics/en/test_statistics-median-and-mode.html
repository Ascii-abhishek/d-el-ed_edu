Section 2: Short Answer

1. Find the median of 6, 4, 9, 3, 8.

Arrange the data: 3, 4, 6, 8, 9

\(n=5\), so median is the 3rd term.

Median = 6

2. Find the mode of 5, 7, 5, 8, 7, 5, 9.

5 occurs 3 times, 7 occurs 2 times, others occur once.

Mode = 5

Section 3: Long Answer

1. Explain the difference between median and mode with one example each.

Median is the middle value after arranging the data in order. Example: In 2, 4, 5, 7, 9 the median is 5.

Mode is the value that occurs most frequently. Example: In 3, 4, 4, 5, 6 the mode is 4.

Median needs arranged data, while mode depends on repetition.
