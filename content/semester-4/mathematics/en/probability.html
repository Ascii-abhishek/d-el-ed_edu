Probability

One of the most visible Semester 4 scoring chapters. UP D.El.Ed papers commonly ask about coin toss, single die, two dice, and sample space. Keep the formula and logic clear.

Main Formula

\[ P(E) = \frac{\text{Number of favourable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of equally likely outcomes}} \]

1. Coin Toss

  • For one coin: \(S = \{H, T\}\)
  • Probability of head \(= \frac{1}{2}\)
  • Probability of tail \(= \frac{1}{2}\)

If two coins are tossed:

\[ S = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\} \]

  • Probability of getting exactly one head \(= \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}\)
  • Probability of getting two heads \(= \frac{1}{4}\)

2. One Die

\[ S = \{1,2,3,4,5,6\} \]

  • Even number: \(\{2,4,6\}\), so \(P(\text{even}) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}\)
  • Prime number: \(\{2,3,5\}\), so \(P(\text{prime}) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}\)

3. Two Dice

Two dice give \(6 \times 6 = 36\) equally likely outcomes.

Example: Sum = 4

\[ (1,3), (2,2), (3,1) \]

So,

\[ P(\text{sum } 4) = \frac{3}{36} = \frac{1}{12} \]

4. Complementary Event

If probability of an event is \(P(E)\), then probability that it does not happen is:

\[ P(E') = 1 - P(E) \]

Example: If \(P(E) = \frac{4}{7}\), then \(P(E') = 1 - \frac{4}{7} = \frac{3}{7}\).

5. Three Coins Example

\[ S = \{HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT\} \]

  • Total outcomes = 8
  • Exactly two heads: \(\{HHT, HTH, THH\}\)
  • \(P(\text{exactly two heads}) = \frac{3}{8}\)

Common Mistakes

  • For two dice, students wrongly take total outcomes as 12. Correct total = 36.
  • Do not mix ordered pairs. \((1,3)\) and \((3,1)\) are different outcomes.
  • Complement means “not that event”, not “opposite number”.

Classroom Connection

  • Use real coins and dice in class so children see randomness physically.
  • Ask students to record 20 throws and compare experimental results with theoretical probability.
  • Connect probability to everyday guesses: rain chance, selecting cards, classroom games.

One-Minute Revision

  • \(P(E) = \frac{\text{favourable}}{\text{total}}\)
  • One die: total outcomes = 6
  • Two dice: total outcomes = 36
  • Two coins: total outcomes = 4
  • Complement: \(P(E') = 1 - P(E)\)
